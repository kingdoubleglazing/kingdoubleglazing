# Local SEO Strategy

## Executive summary

This report outlines a practical, Melbourne-specific local SEO and off‑page strategy for King Double Glazing, a retrofit double glazing and glazing business serving all Melbourne suburbs. It covers Google Business Profile (GBP) optimisation for glaziers, Australian citation building, link acquisition tactics for Melbourne trades, review systems, and schema implementation to support rich results. Where exact live SERP or GBP data is not directly observable, the report draws on current local SEO studies and Australian directory research, and makes conservative, clearly sign‑posted inferences.[1][2]

## Google Business Profile optimisation for Melbourne glaziers

### Primary and secondary categories

Google’s category list includes specific categories relevant to glazing: **“Glazier,” “Glass repair service,” “Glass shop,” “Glass & mirror shop,” “Window installation service,” and related glass merchants categories**. Local search ranking factor studies consistently find that **primary GBP category is the single most influential factor for Map Pack visibility**, with additional categories also contributing.[3][4][5][6][2][7]

Recommended setup for King Double Glazing:

- **Primary category**
    - Glazier (captures broad glazing and glass repair intent for Melbourne).
- **Core secondary categories** (depending on actual services):
    - Glass repair service (emergency breakage, replacements).[4][3]
    - Window installation service (for retrofit or replacement double glazed windows).[8]
    - Door supplier (if supplying/uPVC doors).[9]
    - Glass & mirror shop or Glass shop (if there is a showroom or takeaway glass products).[4]
    - Double glazing installer is often not a standalone category; most double glazing firms use combinations of the above, which align well with current category lists.[10][11]

The key is to **keep categories tightly aligned to real services**, limiting to perhaps 3–5 total, and to push all other detail (retrofit, acoustic glass, shower screens, office partitions) into the Services list and website content.[12][13]

### High‑impact GBP features for trades

Local ranking factor surveys and GBP feature analyses show that for service‑area and trades businesses, the following GBP elements drive both rankings and conversions:[1][14][2]

- **Core profile completeness** – accurate NAP, hours, service area, website, and business description; completeness is itself a ranking signal.[2][15]
- **Reviews and ratings** – volume, velocity (steady new reviews), rating score and owner response rate are now weighted at roughly **20% of local pack ranking influence**, up from about 16% in earlier years.[16][2]
- **Photos and videos** – GBP studies show strong correlation between rich photo libraries and higher clicks, calls and direction requests, particularly for home services.[17][18]
- **Posts** – regular Posts act as a “mini‑billboard” on the profile, highlighting offers, seasonal campaigns and project spotlights.[19][1]
- **Q&A** – well‑seeded Q&A increases conversion and can capture long‑tail retrofit double glazing questions directly in the SERP.[20][1]
- **Messaging / request a quote** – messaging, WhatsApp integration and quote request flows for trades significantly reduce friction and increase enquiries when enabled and monitored.[21][13][22]
- **Attributes & credentials** – business attributes such as years in business, emergency service, online estimates and on‑site services are surfaced as visual “trust badges” and filters.[23][24][21]

For a glazing business that does emergency repairs and planned retrofit work across Melbourne, **messaging/quote**, **attributes**, **photos** and **reviews** should be treated as top‑tier features, not “nice to have”.

### Photos: volume and types

Google officially recommends “at least three photos” for each major product or service type, and highlights logos, cover photos and business photos as core categories. Recent GBP analyses, including Australian‑focused guidance, show:[25][26]

- Businesses with **15+ photos** per location see materially stronger engagement across clicks, calls and direction requests, with home services among the highest photo‑using sectors.[18]
- Independent studies of high‑performing GBPs find **profiles in the top three positions often have 100–250+ images**, compared with 170 or fewer for businesses ranking 11–20.[27][17]

For King Double Glazing a realistic target is:

- **Initial baseline**: 20–40 high‑quality images within the first month (exterior signage, vans, team at work, before‑and‑after retrofits, profiles of double glazed units, close‑ups of frames and seals, office/warehouse).[26][27]
- **Medium term**: Grow to 80–150 images over several months by adding:
    - Project galleries by suburb (e.g. “Retrofit double glazing – Hawthorn,” “Noise‑reduction project – Brunswick”).
    - Service‑type albums (uPVC windows, timber retrofit, sliding doors, office partitions, emergency makesafe boards).
    - User‑generated photos encouraged in review requests.

Emphasis should be on **real, current work in Melbourne suburbs**, which both Google’s guidance and industry studies indicate drives more interactions than stock‑style images.[21][20]

### GBP attributes that matter most for glaziers

Google emphasises **service‑area configuration, credentials and service attributes** for trades and other non‑storefront providers. Trade‑focused GBP optimisation guides and contractor SEO content highlight the attributes that consistently appear on high‑performing profiles:[44][24][45][23][21]

- **Service‑related attributes**
    - On‑site services
    - Online estimates / online appointments
    - Emergency service / 24‑hour service (if genuinely offered)
    - In‑store pick‑up or showroom visits (if a physical display space exists)
- **Operational attributes**
    - Years in business (longevity is called out in Google’s own service‑area business guidance).[21]
    - Appointment required / walk‑ins welcome
    - Service area suburbs and radius for greater Melbourne.
- **Trust & accessibility** (use where accurate)

For King Double Glazing, the **bare minimum** attribute setup should include “service‑area business,” “on‑site services,” “online estimates or quote requests,” correct operating hours, and any relevant emergency or after‑hours attributes, supported by clear messaging about licensing and insurance.

### Best practices for generating reviews in Australia

Local SEO and platform guidance suggest the following best practices for Australian trades businesses seeking reviews:[44][1][20][21]

- **Build review requests into the job workflow** – send a personalised SMS or email with a direct Google review link after completion and inspection, ideally the same day.
- **Ask specifically for detail** – prompt customers to mention suburb, problem (e.g., noise from tram line, heat loss, security), and service type (retrofit double glazing of existing timber windows, replacement uPVC windows, etc.).
- **Encourage photos** – Google highlights customer photos, and GBP guidance plus case studies underline the impact of user‑generated photos on trust and engagement.[26][27][18]
- **Respond to every review** – owner responses are encouraged by Google and seen as part of engagement and trust; responses should address specific details and, where necessary, offer remediation.[44][21]
- **Avoid incentives that breach policy** – offering discounts or gifts expressly in exchange for reviews can violate Google and platform guidelines; instead, focus on strong service and clear, easy review flows.

A simple approach is to **tag each project in your CRM by suburb and service type**, then trigger tailored review request templates mentioning the specific benefit delivered (e.g. “quieter bedroom in Brunswick thanks to acoustic double glazing”).

## Schema markup

### Most valuable schema types for a glazier

[Schema.org](http://schema.org/) provides several types that map well to a Melbourne glazing business:[111][112][8]

- **LocalBusiness** – the base type for a local service provider, with fields for name, address, geo coordinates, opening hours, contact details and sameAs profiles.[112]
- **HomeAndConstructionBusiness** – a subtype of LocalBusiness specifically for businesses providing construction‑related services around homes and buildings (appropriate for glaziers and double glazing installers).[8]
- **Service** – for describing specific services such as “Retrofit double glazing,” “Emergency glass repair,” or “Shower screen installation,” including areaServed (Melbourne, Victoria), serviceType and offers.[8]
- **WebSite and WebPage** – site‑wide and page‑specific markup to help search engines understand the structure of the site and support sitelinks and breadcrumb features.[113][111]
- **Review and AggregateRating** – embedded in LocalBusiness or Product/Service markup where you can legitimately summarise first‑party reviews and ratings.[112][8]
- **FAQPage** – for pages that present retrofit or pricing FAQs in a Q&A format, making them eligible for FAQ rich results when Google still surfaces them for relevant queries.[111]

Australian schema generators and validators emphasise **JSON‑LD format** placed in the head (or body) and highlight that correct LocalBusiness schema with ratings and services improves eligibility for rich results, not guaranteed rankings by itself.[114][115]

### Are Melbourne competitors using schema effectively?

The available tools provide access to rendered page text rather than full HTML, so competitor schema implementations on specific Melbourne glazier sites cannot be comprehensively audited here. However, several observations can still guide a competitive approach:

- Australian SEO tools and agencies offering schema generators strongly promote LocalBusiness / HomeAndConstructionBusiness schema for local service providers, indicating that adoption among sophisticated competitors is growing but still uneven among smaller trades.[115][116][114]
- Many glazing and double glazing sites analysed in content (e.g. Premium Glass & Aluminium, Nu‑Eco, RetroFit Double Glaze, APS Double Glazing) have clearly structured service pages and review claims, but no reference in their visible copy to structured data or rich result features.[30][29][42][34]

Given typical adoption patterns in Australian local SEO, it is reasonable to assume that **some leading competitors use at least basic Organization/LocalBusiness schema**, but **comprehensive HomeAndConstructionBusiness + Service + FAQ markup is not yet universal**. Implementing robust, validated schema for King Double Glazing is therefore likely to provide an incremental edge in SERP appearance and AI summarisation, even though direct competitor markup cannot be fully enumerated from available data.

### Available rich results for glazing/trades queries

[Schema.org](http://schema.org/) and search documentation indicate several rich result types that can apply to glazing and trades businesses when properly marked up and when Google chooses to show them:[114][115][111][8]

- **LocalBusiness panels / knowledge panels** – pulled primarily from GBP and LocalBusiness data, showing name, rating, address, hours, photos and reviews for branded and local queries.
- **Review snippets** – star ratings and review counts on organic listings when LocalBusiness or Product/Service entities include AggregateRating and Review markup that meets Google’s policies.[112][8]
- **FAQ rich results** – expandable Q&A directly in the SERP for pages marked as FAQPage, often used for cost, installation and maintenance questions in home improvement verticals.[111]
- **Sitelinks and breadcrumb trails** – supported by WebSite and WebPage markup and a clean site architecture, helping users jump directly to services like “Retrofit double glazing” or “Emergency glass repair.”[113][111]

Local search statistics also emphasise that **rich SERP elements (Map Pack, reviews, FAQ, sitelinks) materially increase click‑through rates**, even when the underlying ranking position is similar.[7][2][114]

For King Double Glazing, priority schema work should therefore focus on:

- A **core LocalBusiness/HomeAndConstructionBusiness entity**, linked from every page.
- Separate **Service entities** for key offerings (retrofit double glazing, uPVC windows, emergency glass repair, office partitions), each connected to the main business.
- **FAQPage markup** on high‑value educational pages (cost guides, noise reduction, condensation, retrofit vs replacement), aligned with People Also Ask patterns from Australian SERPs.
- Carefully implemented **Review/AggregateRating** data based on real first‑party reviews to support legitimate review snippets.
