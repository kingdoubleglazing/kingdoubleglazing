# Competitor SEO Deep Dive

## Executive Summary

The Melbourne double glazing and glass retrofit market is fragmented between specialist retrofitters (Thermawood, Retrofit Double Glaze), national secondary glazing brands (Magnetite, Stop Noise), uPVC replacement specialists (APS Double Glazing, Windows Republic), and a broad glazier with retrofit capability (Premium Glass & Aluminium). Retrofit Double Glaze, Thermawood, APS Double Glazing, and Windows Republic currently show the most developed content and local SEO footprints, while Magnetite dominates national authority on secondary glazing. There is a clear opportunity for King Double Glazing to combine strong Melbourne-local targeting with structured cost calculators, technical comparison content, and niche intent pages (heritage overlays, body corporate approvals, rental properties) that competitors under-serve.[1][2][3][4]

---

## Market Overview

Melbourne’s climate (cold winters, hot summers, and high energy prices) and the large stock of older timber-window housing stock drives strong demand for retrofit double glazing and secondary glazing solutions. Retrofit solutions such as Thermawood and Retrofit Double Glaze focus on preserving timber frames while improving thermal and acoustic performance, whereas uPVC specialists such as APS Double Glazing and Windows Republic emphasise full-frame replacement with modern multi‑chamber uPVC systems. Secondary glazing brands such as Magnetite and Stop Noise target noise reduction first, positioning secondary glazing as a less disruptive alternative for strata, apartments, and heritage buildings.[5][6][2][7][8][9][10][11][12][13][14]

Common search intents visible across competitor sites and third‑party guides include:

- "double glazed windows Melbourne", "double glazing Melbourne", and "retrofit double glazing Melbourne".
- Location + intent combinations such as "double glazed windows Burwood", "double glazed windows Springvale", "double glazing Clyde".[15][16][17]
- Cost and comparison intents such as "double glazing cost Melbourne", "double glazing price per m2", and "secondary vs double glazing".[18][19][20][14]

## Magnetite ([magnetite.com.au](http://magnetite.com.au/))

### Positioning & Product Model – Secondary Glazing

Magnetite is a national retrofit double glazing brand specialising in **secondary glazing**: a magnetic acrylic panel installed on the inside of existing windows to create a double‑glazed air cavity without replacing original glass or frames.[7][4][11]

- The system installs a slim subframe with steel tape around the reveal; an optical‑grade acrylic panel attaches magnetically, forming an airtight cavity that mimics double glazing.[43][11]
- This creates a larger air gap than typical IGUs, which Magnetite and related technical documentation claim can improve low‑frequency noise reduction, while using acrylic that is lighter and more thermally insulating than glass.[44][45]
- Melbourne is served by a local franchise based in Airport West, covering broader regions including Geelong, Ballarat, and Dandenong.[3][7]

**Secondary glazing vs true retrofit double glazing:**

- Secondary glazing (Magnetite, Stop Noise) adds an internal pane and relies on the original window’s condition, seals, and drainage; it is often favoured where windows cannot be replaced (heritage, strata) and can be installed with less disruption.[19][46]
- True retrofit double glazing (Thermawood, Retrofit Double Glaze, APS, Windows Republic) replaces glass and often modifies or replaces frames to accommodate sealed IGUs, delivering better control over air leakage, drainage, and overall system performance at higher cost and disruption.[20][46][12]

### Content & Traffic Drivers

Key Magnetite content that likely attracts organic traffic includes:

- National **"Magnetite Retrofit Double Glazing"** product page explaining acoustic, thermal, and condensation benefits, with claims of up to 70% noise reduction and significant improvements in window thermal resistance.[45][11]
- **Melbourne location page** that stresses local operations in Airport West, case studies (e.g., Citi Club/RACV building, Bundoora Homestead), and service areas (Geelong, Ballarat, Dandenong).[3]
- Multiple **blog posts** and content hubs on working from home comfort, noise mitigation, and case studies across commercial and residential installations.[47]
- External authority signals, including [ProductReview.com.au](http://productreview.com.au/) and numerous case‑study references, indicate strong national brand recognition.[48]

### Pricing & Offers

Magnetite does not disclose formal per‑square‑metre or per‑opening pricing on public pages; costs are typically referenced in reviews and third‑party discussions as being roughly two‑thirds of full double‑glazing replacement in some scenarios, but with wide variance by project. The standard call‑to‑action is a free measure and quote via each franchise.[49][7][11]

### Reputation & Reviews

- Magnetite holds a **4.7‑star rating from 370 reviews** on [ProductReview.com.au](http://productreview.com.au/), positioning it as one of the most reviewed and best‑rated window retrofit brands in Australia.[48]
- Reviews emphasise strong noise reduction performance and noticeable improvements in thermal comfort, but some customers mention long lead times and occasional condensation or design trade‑offs.[48]

### Strengths

- Clear **national brand authority** with substantial third‑party reviews and longstanding presence since the late 1990s.[50][48]
- Distinct **secondary glazing solution** with strong narrative on noise reduction and minimal disruption, suited to apartments and heritage buildings where frame replacement is difficult.[45][11]
- Case studies across commercial properties provide social proof for institutional and B2B clients.[7]

### Weaknesses & Content Gaps

- Less emphasis on local Melbourne suburb SEO; the Melbourne location page is broad rather than suburb‑specific.[3]
- Limited transparent pricing content, reducing visibility for cost‑oriented searches and comparisons.[49]
- Some consumer confusion between secondary glazing and true double glazing; only limited on‑site content explains detailed trade‑offs, which competing content marketers (e.g., Windows Republic, independent blogs) exploit.[46][19][20]

## Stop Noise ([stopnoise.com.au](http://stopnoise.com.au/))

### Positioning – Noise‑First Secondary Glazing

Stop Noise is a Melbourne‑based family business specialising in **secondary glazing for noise reduction**, explicitly leading with soundproofing outcomes rather than thermal benefits. The core promise is minimum 50% noise reduction, with claims of 50–70% reduction in external noise through retrofitted secondary glazing on existing windows and doors.[6][5]

The service is marketed heavily to:

- Homeowners on busy roads, near trains or flight paths.
- Apartments and strata situations where body‑corporate‑friendly, non‑external‑change solutions are needed.[5]
- Commercial clients such as hotels and motels, where Stop Noise emphasises better TripAdvisor/Google review scores through improved soundproofing.[51]

### Content & Traffic Drivers

Key content elements:

- **Homepage** describing noise reduction claims, secondary glazing approach, and online quote promise: customers can submit window sizes and receive a quote within 24 hours, which functions as a fast manual quote system rather than an automated calculator.[5]
- **Service pages** for soundproof windows and doors, retrofit double glazing, and general soundproofing in Melbourne.[5]
- **Hotel & motel soundproofing** content focusing on review scores and guest satisfaction, clearly targeting noise‑driven B2B leads.[51]
- Blog‑style "Retrofit Double Glazing vs Secondary Glazing" content explaining that secondary glazing can achieve slightly higher noise reduction where larger cavities are possible, especially for certain noise frequencies.[52]

### Pricing & Offers

Stop Noise does not publish explicit price tables; instead, it frames its system as **"half the cost of replacement double glazed windows"** and offers free quotes based on submitted measurements. The emphasis is on value vs full replacement and on rapid quote turnaround.[5]

### Reputation & Reviews

Publicly crawlable sources show client testimonials and references to long operating history (since 1995) with 29+ years in the industry, but Google review counts and averages are not readily accessible via HTML. Stop Noise is mentioned positively in renovation forums as a more affordable alternative to full double glazing for noise and condensation issues.[31][5]

### Strengths

- **Highly focused noise positioning**; messaging repeatedly reinforces 50–70% noise reduction and features case narratives around traffic noise and apartment soundproofing.[6][52][5]
- Uses **fast online quote workflow** (24‑hour quote based on dimensions) as a UX advantage over slower measure‑and‑quote only competitors.[5]
- Strong appeal where **body corporate or heritage constraints** limit visible external changes.

### Weaknesses & Content Gaps

- Limited educational content on thermal performance, U‑values, and energy savings compared to APS and Windows Republic; thermal messaging is secondary.[6][5]
- Sparse suburb‑specific landing pages and case studies relative to APS and Retrofit Double Glaze.
- No pricing tables or per‑m² guidance; cost messaging is qualitative.

## Opportunity Gaps – Keywords & Content

While exact keyword volumes and rankings require direct access to tools such as Ahrefs or Semrush, patterns in current competitor content and external cost guides reveal several under‑served topics and intents.

### Under‑Served Content Themes

1. **Interactive pricing / calculators**
    - No analysed Melbourne double‑glazing competitor offers a live price calculator where users can configure window types, sizes, and options for instant estimates; all rely on static guides or manual quotes.[33][66][5][9]
    - Other Australian and UK glazing companies (e.g., Arco Double Glazing in Perth) do provide instant estimators, validating user demand for this feature.[80]
    - A well‑designed estimator calibrated to realistic APS/Windows Republic/APS pricing bands could form a strong SEO and UX differentiator for King Double Glazing.
2. **Heritage overlays, strata and body‑corporate approvals (Melbourne‑specific)**
    - Thermawood references heritage suitability, but there is limited in‑depth content on navigating local planning rules, heritage overlays, and body corporate approvals in Melbourne.[40][12]
    - Dedicated guides and checklists ("double glazing in heritage overlays in [council]", "body corporate rules for window upgrades in Melbourne apartments") would fill a real gap.
3. **Detailed performance comparisons with data**
    - Windows Republic and APS explain U‑values and Low‑E coatings, and Magnetite provides lab data, but there are few side‑by‑side comparison tables of **secondary vs retrofit vs full replacement** with realistic noise and thermal outcomes in Melbourne climates.[19][45][20]
    - Independent‑style comparison content (with clear disclaimers) would attract research‑mode customers and backlinks.
4. **Use‑case‑driven content**
    - Competitors mostly structure content by product (sliding windows, doors) or benefits (noise, thermal). There is less content framed around **specific user scenarios** such as:
        - "Double glazing for renters and landlord approvals".
