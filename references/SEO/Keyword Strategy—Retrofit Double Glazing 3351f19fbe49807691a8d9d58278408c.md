# Keyword Strategy—Retrofit Double Glazing

Executive Summary

Retrofit and replacement double glazing is a growing search vertical in Australia, driven by energy prices, comfort concerns, and increasing awareness of window performance. Search demand is strongest around generic "double glazing" and Melbourne‑specific service terms, with retrofit and cost‑oriented queries forming valuable but smaller long‑tail clusters. Public web sources do not expose precise, per‑keyword Australian monthly search volumes; only directionally consistent demand and seasonal trends can be inferred without access to closed tools such as Google Ads Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, or Semrush. For this reason, this report classifies demand using qualitative tiers (High/Medium/Low) rather than fabricated numeric volumes and recommends that final targeting numbers be validated in a keyword tool.[1][2][3][4][5]

The most important strategic implication is that King Double Glazing should dominate retrofit‑specific, value‑driven intent ("double glazing existing windows", "retrofit double glazing cost", "double glazing without replacing frames") while still competing selectively on broader "double glazing Melbourne" terms using strong local signals and differentiated messaging around price per square metre and frame‑preserving installation.[6][7][8]

## Retrofit Double Glazing Cluster

### Cluster Overview

Retrofit double glazing in Australia typically means removing existing single panes and installing a modern insulated glass unit (IGU) into the existing timber or aluminium frames, or adding secondary glazing systems to existing windows. This approach is framed as less disruptive and significantly cheaper than full frame replacement, with Australian pricing guides and service providers often quoting retrofit in the range of roughly one‑third to one‑half of full replacement window projects. Melbourne retrofit specialists highlight benefits such as preserving heritage timber frames, improving thermal and acoustic performance, and keeping costs closer to 500–600 per square metre rather than 1,200–2,400 per square metre for full replacement.[10][11][12][8][13][28][29][30][9][6]

For King Double Glazing, this cluster should be the **core topical silo**, with the homepage, main retrofit service page, and several supporting blog posts and FAQs all interlinked.

## Double Glazing Cost and Pricing Cluster

### Cost Cluster Overview

Australian homeowners consistently search for the cost of double glazing in terms of both total project price and per square metre rates, and many Australian guides explicitly state typical cost ranges to educate consumers. National and Melbourne‑specific content frequently quotes double glazing replacement at roughly 300–1,500 per square metre depending on frame material, with full house projects often ranging from around 8,000 to 40,000 plus. Retrofit and secondary glazing are commonly positioned in guides as cheaper alternatives at roughly 500–600 per square metre, whereas full replacement in timber or thermally broken aluminium frames can be 1,200–2,800 per square metre.[11][35][36][34][10][13][9][12]

King Double Glazing’s clear retrofit pricing at 495 per square metre sits at the lower end of the retrofit ranges given by Australian cost guides, making **price‑based keywords strategically important** for positioning against competitors and national cost articles.[38][10][11][9][12]

## Seasonal Search Trends for Double Glazing in Australia

Open‑web analyses of Google search data show **rising overall interest in double glazing across Australia over the last several years**, with some content noting peaks in mid‑year periods. One trends‑oriented article describes a spike in search interest for specific double glazed window types in October, suggesting that renovation and marketing cycles can cause short‑term peaks outside of winter as well.[2][4]

Australian climate and energy‑efficiency articles consistently stress winter comfort and energy savings as primary motivations, indicating that research and purchase intent are particularly strong ahead of and during colder months. However, several guides emphasise that double glazing also mitigates summer heat and year‑round energy use, so interest persists across all seasons rather than disappearing in warmer months.[45][46][4][3][33][42][5]

For Melbourne and Victoria specifically:

- Homeowners are encouraged to think about insulation and window performance before winter, and some installers position winter and late autumn as strong times for upgrades.[45][3]
- National and Sydney‑focused content indicates that double glazing is a year‑round comfort and energy‑efficiency upgrade, not just a winter fix, but marketing emphasis often intensifies in autumn and winter.[46][4][45]

The practical implication is to **front‑load campaigns and new content releases in late summer and autumn**, while keeping evergreen informational content updated year‑round.
